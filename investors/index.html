The Co-ordination Gap

Volume is the enemy of co-ordination.

Even a single channel of simultaneous volunteer updates creates co-ordination overload. Multiple channels make it even harder to manage.

Most community-led searches are co-ordinated without purpose-built tools. The result is duplicated ground, missed areas, coordinator overload, and no verifiable record of what was searched. This is not a resource problem — it is a co-ordination problem. GRIDSEARCH is the structured alternative.

  • 01
    No Verified Coverage
    Without GPS-verified sector tracking, critical areas are missed and ground is duplicated. After the search, there is nothing to review, learn from, or present at inquest.
  • 02
    No Volunteer Safety Net
    No existing tool monitors volunteers working alone in difficult terrain. No fall detection, no inactivity alerts, no emergency function. If a volunteer goes down, no one knows.
  • 03
    No Legal Protection
    When a search results in a fatality or inquest, informal records cannot substitute for a structured, court-admissible audit trail. Organisations are legally exposed.
  • 04
    No Animal Search Capability
    Not one platform in either market has ever been designed to support animal searches. An entire segment — canine rescue, wildlife response, agricultural search — has no co-ordination software at all.
Platform Architecture

A Decision Support System. Not an autonomous one.

Coordinators retain full operational authority at all times.
GRIDSEARCH provides structure, intelligence, and accountability — not control.
Operational in minutes from any device. Zero training required by design.

📋
One-Tap Field Reporting
Buttons for Possible Sighting, Confirmed Sighting, Report Clue, Report Hazard, Request Drone, and Need Help. Integrated photo capture with automatic GPS tagging and timestamping. Voice-to-text dictation processed by an AI engine that categorises reports automatically. Designed with 64×64 dp touch targets for use in the field — in any weather, on any device, with no training required. Utilises an automated client-side compression protocol to ensure the platform remains light and performant during high-volume incidents.
◉
Volunteer Safety & Hard-Cut Protocol
GPS tracking is programmatically severed upon 'End Search'. This is an architectural enforcement — not a configurable setting. Active-state tracking operates only between 'Start Search' and 'End Search'. Fall detection, inactivity alerts, and an SOS emergency button are active throughout. If a fall is detected, co-ordinators are notified immediately with last known coordinates. Identity data and GPS tracks are siloed in separate encrypted tables, linked only by an anonymous ID.
⚖
Court-Admissible Audit Trail
Every platform action generates an automatic, timestamped, immutable log entry. Log entries cannot be edited or deleted — only annotations may be appended, and annotations are themselves logged. Each entry carries a cryptographic hash: any tampering is detectable on export. The export format is structured for legal proceedings, inquests, and liability reviews in PDF and JSON/XML formats. UTC-standardised throughout for cross-border legal consistency.
📡
Multi-Constellation Positioning & Connectivity Resilience
Simultaneously utilises GPS, Galileo, and GLONASS for location reliability in challenging terrain. Burst Sync Architecture: data is stored locally and synchronises upon signal return. Designed for Satellite Direct-to-Cell (D2C) readiness — when carrier-enabled satellite D2C coverage is available on a volunteer's device, the platform inherits it transparently with no rebuild required. Thermal throttling at 40°C+ prevents hardware shutdown.
⊞
Probabilistic Search Engine & Golden Co-ordinate Protocol
Each incident loads a Subject Profile and the engine calculates a Probability of Area (POA) score per sector, based on last known point, terrain type, and time elapsed since last contact. Sectors are colour-coded dynamically. Clue integration continuously redistributes probability without manual intervention. Advisory only — the coordinator may override at any time.

At incident closure, the platform mandates capture of the Golden Co-ordinate — the verified location at which the subject was found. Subject State Metadata and Discovery Method tagging are logged, feeding an ML pipeline that iteratively calibrates the probabilistic models against ground-truth data. Each deployment makes the engine more accurate.
◈
Modular Subject Profile Libraries
Proprietary data modules — each a structured dataset of behaviour parameters, travel distance distributions, terrain preference weightings, and time-decay curves. Libraries are loaded into the Probabilistic Scoring Engine at runtime based on the organisation's unlocked library set. The engine is grounded in established Lost Person Behaviour research. Adding a new library post-launch requires only a new data module — no platform rebuild. The architecture supports modular behavioural datasets to be developed and unlocked progressively beyond the MVP, with the full library purchase flow activated in Phase 3.

What GRIDSEARCH combines.

Global First
Modular Subject Profile Libraries
No SAR platform has ever applied probabilistic scoring beyond human subjects. GRIDSEARCH extends the same engine to a proprietary library of non-human subject profiles — to be developed progressively beyond the MVP. This directly serves segments — canine rescue, wildlife response, agricultural search — that have never had purpose-built co-ordination software.
By Architecture
Telemetry Hard-Cut & Volunteer Privacy
Every other platform tracks users for as long as the session runs with no automatic cutoff. GRIDSEARCH severs GPS telemetry the instant a volunteer presses End Search. Identity data and GPS tracks are stored in siloed, encrypted tables, linked only by an anonymous ID. This is an architectural enforcement — not a configurable setting. The only platform a privacy-conscious volunteer organisation can adopt without legal exposure.
Day One
Court-Admissible Audit Trail
Every professional SAR platform has this. No volunteer tool does. GRIDSEARCH includes it from the MVP — immutable, hash-verified, UTC-standardised, and structured for legal proceedings. When a search ends in a fatality or inquest, the organisation has a complete, tamper-evident record. Structured for export in formats meeting agency handover requirements at Phase 3.
Unique
Family & Owner Transparency View
Every professional tool excludes families by design. Every volunteer tool provides no structured view at all. GRIDSEARCH gives families and animal owners a read-only map showing verified search coverage — the documented answer to the question they are asking. Access is revoked automatically when the session ends. No access to volunteer identities or internal reports at any point.
